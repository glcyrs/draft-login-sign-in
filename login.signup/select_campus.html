About Batangas State University

Batangas State University (BatStateU) is a top Philippine higher learning institution known for its excellence in education, research, and community service. It offers programs in engineering, architecture, business, agriculture, environmental science, information technology, and education. The university has multiple campuses, including Lobo, Alangilan, Mabini, and Balayan, each with specialized programs. BatStateU's mission is to advance communities, industries, and the nation through education and innovation.

LOBO CAMPUS

BatStateU Lobo, located in Brgy. Masaguitsuit, is the only College of Agriculture and Forestry in Batangas province and one of the few in CALABARZON. With white sand beaches, protected mangrove forests, fish sanctuaries, and marine protected areas, Lobo's main industries include agriculture and food production. Its seas are part of the Verde Island Passage, known for its marine shorefish biodiversity.

ALANGILAN CAMPUS

Alangilan, the second oldest campus of the university, was acquired in 1984 and is located in Golden Country Homes, Batangas City. It covers 5.62 hectares, with three colleges and research hubs occupying 2.89 hectares. Recently, 2.73 hectares were acquired for the Knowledge, Innovation, and Science Technology (KIST) Park.

MABINI CAMPUS

BatStateU Mabini, the newest campus, opened in 2018 with the support of the local government and the Yu family. The campus, located on a four-hectare property, features a three-storey Josefina L. Yu Hall as its first academic infrastructure. Mabini's thriving economy and diving destinations make it a hub for business.

BALAYAN CAMPUS

Batangas State University Balayan, established in 1994, is a community-oriented institution in Brgy. Caloocan, Balayan, offering technology and technical-vocational education programs to the youth of the community, a first-class municipality with numerous industries and small and medium-scale enterprises.
